Planning Comparison

Two paths, very different family experiences

The numbers matter, but the real planning question is what happens around the family when care decisions arrive.

Without a strategy

The default path

Care costs are handled under pressure, often by drawing from the assets the estate plan was meant to preserve.

1

Portfolio and liquid reserves become the first line of defense.

2

Forced liquidation can happen when timing is unfavorable.

3

Spouse, heirs, and adult children absorb more coordination stress.

4

Estate documents may be technically sound but financially strained.

With a strategy

The prepared path

A dedicated plan gives the family a funding roadmap before health, legal, and financial decisions collide.

1

Care funding is planned before the crisis begins.

2

More assets can remain aligned with spouse, heir, and legacy goals.

3

Adult children get clearer roles and fewer emergency decisions.

4

Attorney and advisor work from the same family protection map.
